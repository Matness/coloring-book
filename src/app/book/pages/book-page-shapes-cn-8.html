These shapes that share a cyclic group have the same group of rotations that leave their appearance unchanged. For example, the shapes of C3 all have the rotations {0 turn, 1/3 turn, 2/3 turn } and no more unique rotations other than those.

Our shapes help us see our groups, but the members of the groups are the rotations, not the shapes.
